Post-Stroke Exercises

Introduction to Post-Stroke Exercises

After experiencing a stroke, regular physical activity and exercises can play a crucial role in recovery and improving quality of life. Here are some important exercises:

Exercises for Stroke Recovery

  • Range of Motion Exercises: Gentle stretching and movement exercises to improve flexibility and prevent muscle stiffness.
  • Strength Training: Gradual strengthening exercises for affected muscles to regain strength and endurance.
  • Balance and Coordination Exercises: Exercises focusing on improving balance and coordination to reduce the risk of falls.
  • Aerobic Exercises: Low-impact aerobic activities like walking or cycling to improve cardiovascular health.
  • Speech and Language Exercises: Exercises to improve speech and language abilities affected by stroke.

Consult with a healthcare professional or physical therapist before starting any exercise program after a stroke.

Post-Stroke Upper and Lower Limb Exercises

Post-Stroke Upper and Lower Limb Exercises

Upper Limb Exercises

  • Shoulder Flexion and Extension: Raise and lower your affected arm while seated or standing.
  • Bicep Curls: Use light weights or resistance bands to perform bicep curls.
  • Finger and Hand Exercises: Squeeze a stress ball or practice picking up small objects with your fingers.
  • Reach and Grasp Activities: Practice reaching for objects at different heights and distances.
  • Wrist Rotations: Rotate your wrists clockwise and counterclockwise to improve flexibility.

Lower Limb Exercises

  • Leg Raises: Lift your affected leg while lying down to strengthen thigh muscles.
  • Ankle Circles: Rotate your ankles in circles to improve flexibility and circulation.
  • Standing Balance Exercises: Use support and practice standing on one leg to improve balance.
  • Walking Activities: Practice walking with assistive devices, gradually increasing distance and speed.
  • Calf Raises: Rise up on your toes and lower back down to strengthen calf muscles.

Consult with a healthcare professional or physical therapist to customize an exercise program based on your specific needs and abilities.
